Product Safety Culture (PSCI)

The CTU promotes the gaining of a safety culture among the whole medicine development enterprise in order to address the issues line on-going recalls of the products which are approved; post-marketing Risk Evaluation and Mitigation (REMs) requirement; the loss of public trust in the safety of biomedical products and the higher costs of safety settlement and litigation. The need for an entity which is independent is to investigate the incidents of safety and the systemic safety concerns.

The PSCI members Steering Committee have already published the Safety Culture principles in the peer-reviewed journals. In the clinical research where there is the huge amount of the ‘safety evidence’ from the science of organization, it has not been systematically implemented which results in the bad error in management, risk aversion and unsafe practices. The PCSI intended to explore the application of the safety engineering technique in designing the safe systems for clinical research.

First-in-human Trials

The difficult step in the development of the novel therapeutics is the movement from the preclinical laboratory and the studies of animals into the testing of humans. The one project which is under development is a well defined system for the research of first-in-man. The general roadmap for the early evaluation and development of the new potential medicines might be leading to enhancing the information sharing and the benchmarking of the systems which are safety-focused. These approaches will be identifying the gaps between the systems which results from the lack of constraints and controls, which might have imposed the operations and system design.
