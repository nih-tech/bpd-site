Objectives

  • Developing systems and processes that would allow conducting first-in-human clinical trials with an emphasis on rare diseases and diseases of high public health impact
  • Investigating the pathogenesis of disease, queries about new therapies, vaccines or diagnostic procedures
  • Developing state-of-the-art diagnostic, preventive, and therapeutic interventions which are safe and effective
  • Improving access to drugs and health care providers
  • Ensuring that trial staff are consistently supervised across studies, and all tasks are undertaken
  • Training the current and next generations of clinical researchers to meet Good Clinical Practice requirements
  • Ensuring that clinical research is ethical, efficient, and of high scientific quality
  • Delivering projects timely, while ensuring that safety of patients and the credibility of data is maintained.
  • Re-establishing public credibility/trust, especially by: addressing safety, regulatory, operational and performance challenges; and meeting corporate social responsibility (CSR) obligations, legislated or advocated

Our Goals

  • To promote and support clinical research in country by acting as a conduit for networking within the industry.
  • Providing information and resources for the industry
  • Educating stakeholders on issues pertinent to clinical research in country
  • Supporting and encouraging training opportunities for our members
  • Promoting N.I.H as a preferred location for clinical research
